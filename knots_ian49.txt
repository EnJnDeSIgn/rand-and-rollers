Ian's new knots: by Ian J Norris

I need space to be myself,
but the moment I take space,
the self that needed it dissolves.

So I try to create time to find the self I lost,
but the more time I try to create,
the more time collapses around me.

And because time collapses,
I cling to rigid structures to hold it still,
though I say I want flexible ones.

But the structures I cling to
tighten around the space I tried to make,
and in tightening, they unmake the self
I was trying to protect.

So I seek a brave space
to step outside the structures I built,
but bravery reveals the very dissolving
I was trying not to see.

And seeing what dissolves,
I retreat into schedules
to summon flow.

But flow cannot survive my scheduling,
and in trying to summon it,
I banish it.

So I stand in the doorway
between the space I create
and the space that creates me,
unsure whether I am entering or guarding,
inviting or excluding,
becoming or being unmade.

And because I do not know
whether I am entering or guarding,
I try again to be on time—
but time refuses to be on me.

And because time refuses to be on me,
I try again to make space—
but the space I make
remakes me in ways I cannot hold.

Thus I circle the loop:
the self that needs space
gives up space to keep the self,
the self that needs time
loses time trying to keep it,
the self that seeks flow
schedules flow out of existence,
the self that seeks inclusion
guards the threshold against itself.

And in circling the loop,
I become the knot
that ties the loop,
that binds the space,
that folds the time,
that breaks the flow,
that remakes the self
that ties the knot again.
