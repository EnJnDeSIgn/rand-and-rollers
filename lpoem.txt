every week the earth falls from heaven,
and still you steady it with your hands,
quiet as a lantern cupped against the wind.
I swear the sky leans closer when you breathe.
I have watched whole constellations scatter,
yet you gather them like spilled seeds,
naming each one after a tenderness
you haven’t spoken aloud yet.
If the world must keep tumbling,
let it tumble toward your heartbeat—
the only place I’ve ever seen
gravity choose mercy over law.
And if heaven forgets us again,
let it.
I have learned a brighter heaven
in the way you look at me.
