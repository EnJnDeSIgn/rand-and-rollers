Ian's new knots: by Ian J Norris

I need space to be myself,
and when I take space,
I find there is room enough for me.

And finding room enough,
I no longer need to give it away to prove I deserve it.
So the self that once dissolved
begins to take shape again.

And because the self takes shape,
I no longer chase time to hold it together.
Time expands when I stop trying to make it.
And in expanding,
it offers itself freely.

And because time offers itself,
I no longer cling to rigid structures to keep it still.
The structures soften
when I stop gripping them.
And in softening,
they hold me without binding me.

So I step into brave spaces
not to test my courage
but to meet what is already waiting with gentleness.
And what bravery reveals
is not danger but depth.

And seeing depth,
I no longer schedule flow.
Flow returns when I stop summoning it.
And in returning,
it carries me without demanding control.

So I stand in the doorway
not as guard,
not as exile,
but as one who recognizes
the threshold is a place of passage,
not a place of proof.

And because the threshold is passage,
I enter.
And because I enter,
I am included.
And because I am included,
I no longer fear exclusion.

And because I no longer fear exclusion,
time meets me where I am.
And because time meets me,
space opens.
And because space opens,
the self unfolds.

Thus the loop unloops:
the space that once unmade me
now makes room for me,
the time that once collapsed
now stretches,
the flow that once fled
now returns,
the doorway that once barred me
now welcomes me.

And in unlooping the loop,
I become the one
who can hold space without losing self,
who can meet time without chasing it,
who can enter flow without forcing it,
who can cross thresholds
without guarding them.

I become the one
who can untie the knot
without cutting the cord.
