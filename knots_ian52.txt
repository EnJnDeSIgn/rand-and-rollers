Ian's new knots: by Ian J Norris

I need space to be myself,
and when I take space,
I discover a self I did not know I was making.

And discovering that self,
I find that time behaves differently around it—
stretching, folding, spiraling,
as if it were learning me
as I am learning it.

So I stop trying to create time
and begin to listen to how time creates me.
And in listening,
I notice that structure is not a cage
but a chrysalis—
rigid enough to hold the change,
porous enough to let the change through.

And because the chrysalis holds me,
I step into brave spaces
not to prove anything
but to molt.

And in molting,
I shed the schedules that once banished flow.
Flow returns,
but not as a river I must enter—
as wings I did not know I was growing.

So I stand in the doorway
not as guard,
not as exile,
but as one who realizes
the doorway is not a boundary
but a hinge.

And because it is a hinge,
I turn.
And in turning,
I become the one who can be included
without needing to belong,
and belong
without needing to be included.

And because I can turn,
time no longer needs to meet me,
nor I it.
We move toward each other
as two dancers learning the same step
from opposite directions.

And because we move,
space reshapes itself—
not expanding, not contracting,
but reconfiguring
to fit the self that is still forming.

Thus the loop neither binds nor unbinds:
it transforms.

The space that once unmade me
now remakes me differently each time I enter it.
The time that once collapsed
now folds into new dimensions.
The flow that once fled
now changes its course to meet me.
The doorway that once trapped me
now pivots around me.

And in this shifting geometry,
I become the one
who is neither tied nor untied,
but continually re‑tied
into new shapes of being.

I become the knot
that learns itself
by changing.
